About MUN

Model United Nations (MUN) is a simulation of the United Nations where students, known as delegates, participate in conferences that replicate UN committees. The primary purpose of MUN is to provide a platform for students to engage in diplomatic and international relations, as well as to develop their skills in public speaking , critical thinking, research, and negotiation.


Events of SF

SVCE hosted its inaugural intra-college Model United Nations event on June 5th and 6th, 2022, spanning two days. This Model United Nations, commonly known as MUN, aimed to enhance students' understanding of diverse global social events. The competition involved 26 delegates who were assigned specific countries . The platform allowed representatives to pose questions and collaborate in formulating resolutions towards the conclusion of the event


MUN around the world

Held in schools, colleges, and conferences worldwide. Through role-playing as delegates representing different countries. This experiential learning experience not only cultivates a deeper awareness of international relations but also nurtures the next generation of leaders and diplomats who may play a crucial role in shaping the future of our interconnected world.

Why join MUN?

Joining Model United Nations (MUN) offers a unique and invaluable experience for individuals seeking to cultivate their skills in diplomacy, public speaking, and global awareness. Through this immersive and collaborative environment, delegates enhance their research abilities, analytical thinking and the art of persuasion

In what ways will MUN benefit me?

Participating in Model United Nations (MUN) offers numerous benefits that extend beyond the academic realm. It fosters a deep understanding of international relations, geopolitics, and global affairs, broadening your perspective on diverse cultures and perspectives. MUN also cultivates teamwork, as you collaborate with peers to craft resolutions and navigate complex political landscapes. Additionally, the experience hones your research abilities and sharpens your ability to articulate ideas persuasively. These skills acquired in MUN not only contribute to personal development but also prove valuable in various professional and academic pursuits, equipping you with the tools to thrive in a dynamic and interconnected world.

what are the things that Mun can teach me?

Mun can teach you various aspects of economics, including macroeconomic principles, monetary policy, fiscal policy, and international trade. You can learn how to analyze economic data, understand market trends, and make informed decisions in financial matters. Additionally, Mun can provide insights into economic theories, historical contexts, and contemporary issues, enabling you to develop a deeper understanding of the global economy and its complexities. Through simulation exercises and real-world examples, Mun can help you hone your critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making skills in the field of economics.



how to be clear and confident in mun?

Research and Understand Your Country/Delegate

Know your assigned country or delegate's stance on various issues.Understand your country's foreign policy and historical background. Familiarize yourself with the key international treaties and agreements your country has signed

Practice Public Speaking

Practice speaking clearly, confidently, and at a moderate pace. Work on your posture and body language. Practice making concise and relevant points

Be Confident in Q & A Sessions

Be prepared for questions from other delegates. Answer questions confidently, even if you don't have all the information. It's okay to acknowledge when you need to do further research.
